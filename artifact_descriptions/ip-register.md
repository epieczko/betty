# Name: ip-register

## Executive Summary

The Intellectual Property (IP) Register is a comprehensive database documenting all organizational IP assets including patents, trademarks, copyrights, trade secrets, domain names, and proprietary technology. This register serves as the authoritative system of record for IP portfolio management, enabling defensive patent strategies, trademark enforcement, M&A valuation, and licensing revenue optimization. Organizations must track USPTO and international IP registrations, application statuses, renewal deadlines, and ownership chains to prevent inadvertent IP abandonment and maintain enforceability.

Modern IP management platforms (Anaqua, CPA Global, Questel, IPfolio, Alt Legal) have automated IP portfolio tracking with deadline management, prosecution workflow, and analytics dashboards. Enterprise software companies maintain patent portfolios of 1,000-10,000+ patents requiring systematic tracking of filing dates, priority claims, patent families, and geographic coverage across USPTO, EPO, JPO, WIPO PCT filings. The IP register integrates with freedom-to-operate analyses, prior art searches, and defensive publication strategies (e.g., IP.com, Linux Foundation's Open Invention Network).

### Strategic Importance

- **Asset Valuation**: Quantifies IP portfolio value for financial reporting, investor communications, and balance sheet intangible asset recognition
- **M&A Due Diligence**: Accelerates IP verification in acquisitions by providing clean title, prosecution history, and freedom-to-operate documentation
- **Defensive Patent Strategy**: Enables cross-licensing negotiations, patent pool participation, and defensive termination of patent troll litigation
- **Trademark Enforcement**: Tracks trademark registration status, renewal deadlines, and geographic coverage for global brand protection
- **Licensing Revenue**: Identifies monetization opportunities through patent licensing, trademark sublicensing, and technology transfer
- **Prior Art Defense**: Documents invention disclosures, defensive publications, and prior art for patent invalidity defenses
- **Trade Secret Protection**: Maintains confidential information inventory with access controls, NDAs, and employee IP assignment tracking

## Purpose & Scope

### Primary Purpose

This register maintains a centralized, authoritative inventory of all organizational intellectual property assets with ownership documentation, registration status, geographic coverage, renewal deadlines, and valuation data. It enables proactive IP portfolio management, preventing inadvertent abandonment through deadline tracking, supporting licensing negotiations with comprehensive portfolio data, and facilitating M&A due diligence with clean IP title documentation.

### Scope

**In Scope**:
- Patent portfolio: utility patents, design patents, plant patents with USPTO/EPO/JPO/WIPO filing numbers and status
- Patent applications: provisional applications, non-provisional applications, PCT applications, continuation/divisional applications
- Patent families: priority claims, Paris Convention filings, Patent Cooperation Treaty (PCT) international phases
- Trademark registrations: word marks, logos, service marks with ®, ™, ℠ status and Nice Classification classes
- Trademark applications: intent-to-use, use-in-commerce, Madrid Protocol international registrations
- Copyright registrations: software copyright, documentation copyright, with Copyright Office registration numbers
- Domain name portfolio: top-level domains (TLDs), country-code domains (ccTLDs), brand protection registrations
- Trade secrets inventory: confidential algorithms, proprietary processes, customer lists with protection measures documented
- Open-source project trademarks: project marks, logo registrations, community trademark licenses
- Invention disclosure tracking: employee invention disclosures, patent committee decisions, defensive publication elections
- Licensing agreements: patent cross-licenses, trademark sublicenses, royalty-bearing licenses with revenue tracking
- IP ownership chain: employee IP assignments, contractor work-for-hire, acquisition IP transfer documentation

**Out of Scope**:
- Product roadmaps and technology strategies (business planning documents)
- Research and development project details (R&D portfolio management)
- Open-source license compliance tracking (License BOM artifact)
- Trade secret technical content itself (stored in secure repositories, not in register)
- Marketing brand guidelines beyond trademark registration details

### Target Audience

**Primary Audience**:
- Legal Counsel: Patent prosecution, trademark opposition, IP litigation, licensing negotiations, M&A IP due diligence
- IP Management Team: Deadline monitoring, renewal payments, portfolio optimization, abandonment decisions
- Finance: IP asset valuation, intangible asset accounting (ASC 350), licensing revenue recognition
- M&A Team: IP due diligence checklists, clean title verification, encumbrance identification

**Secondary Audience**:
- R&D/Engineering: Invention disclosure submission, prior art searches, freedom-to-operate queries
- Business Development: Patent licensing opportunities, technology transfer prospects, IP monetization strategies
- Risk Management: IP insurance coverage evaluation, patent troll defense preparation, infringement risk assessment
- Investors/Board: IP portfolio reporting, competitive patent landscape, strategic IP asset acquisition rationale

## Best Practices

**Centralized IP Management System**: Use dedicated IP management platform (Anaqua, Alt Legal, IPfolio) rather than spreadsheets for deadline tracking
**Automated Deadline Monitoring**: Configure alerts for patent maintenance fees (3.5, 7.5, 11.5 years USPTO), trademark renewals (5-6 years, 9-10 years)
**Patent Family Tracking**: Document priority claims, Paris Convention filings, and PCT national phase entries to maintain global patent family visibility
**Ownership Chain Documentation**: Maintain employee IP assignments, contractor work-for-hire agreements, and M&A IP transfer documents for clean title
**Invention Disclosure Workflow**: Implement formal invention disclosure process with patent committee review and defensive publication decisions
**Freedom-to-Operate Searches**: Conduct patent landscape searches before product launches to identify blocking patents and design-around opportunities
**USPTO PAIR Integration**: Sync patent prosecution status from Public PAIR to IP register for up-to-date application status and office action tracking
**Trademark Watch Services**: Subscribe to trademark monitoring (MarkMonitor, Corsearch) to detect confusingly similar applications
**Geographic Coverage Analysis**: Map patent/trademark coverage to business markets; prioritize renewals in strategic geographies, abandon in non-key markets
**Portfolio Pruning**: Annual review to identify low-value patents for abandonment before maintenance fee deadlines to optimize portfolio ROI
**Trade Secret Inventory**: Document trade secrets with confidentiality measures, access logs, and employee NDA tracking for DTSA protection
**Licensing Revenue Tracking**: Link licenses to underlying IP assets for royalty reconciliation and revenue recognition (ASC 606)
**Valuation Updates**: Refresh IP portfolio valuation annually or before M&A for accurate intangible asset reporting under ASC 350/IAS 38
**Patent Troll Database**: Maintain list of patent assertion entities (PAEs) and prior litigation history for early identification of troll assertions
**Defensive Publication Strategy**: Publish defensive disclosures (IP.com, Technical Disclosure Commons) for inventions not worth patenting but need prior art blocking
**Open-Source Trademark Governance**: Document open-source project trademark ownership, community usage terms, and fork naming policies
**Domain Name Monitoring**: Track domain registrations containing brand terms; file UDRP/URS complaints for cybersquatting within 60-90 days of discovery
**M&A IP Checklist**: Maintain acquisition IP due diligence template covering chain-of-title, encumbrances, employee assignments, open-source compliance
**Patent Landscape Dashboards**: Visualize competitive patent filings, technology trends, and white space opportunities for R&D strategic planning
**Access Control**: Restrict IP register access to need-to-know basis; trade secret information requires confidentiality agreements and audit logs
